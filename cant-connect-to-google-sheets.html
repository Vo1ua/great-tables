Google Sheets Won't Connect? How to Fix It (Step by Step)

A spreadsheet stuck on "Trying to connect," a blank white screen, or a sudden "network error" message — these are some of the most common (and most frustrating) Google Sheets problems, and they're rarely caused by Google Sheets itself. Most of the time, the real cause is something on your side: your browser, your connection, or your account permissions. Here's how to work through the fixes in order, from the quickest to the more involved.

1. Check Your Internet Connection First

It sounds obvious, but it's the most common cause. Google Sheets is a fully online tool — unless you've set up offline access in advance, it needs a stable connection to load or save anything. Try opening a different website to confirm your connection is actually working, or switch to a different network if one is available.

2. Reload, Then Try Incognito Mode

A simple reload fixes more connection issues than you'd expect. If that doesn't help, open the same spreadsheet in an incognito or private window.

Incognito mode loads the page without your saved extensions, cache, or cookies — if the sheet loads fine there, you've just confirmed the problem is something local to your regular browser profile, not your account or the file itself.

3. Clear Your Browser Cache and Cookies

A buildup of cached data can quietly break how Google Sheets loads, especially after the app has been updated on Google's side.

  1. Click the three-dot menu in your browser's top-right corner.
  2. Go to More tools → Clear browsing data.
  3. Select Cached images and files (and cookies, if the problem persists).
  4. Click Clear data, then reload Google Sheets.

Note that clearing cookies will sign you out of sites you're currently logged into, including Google — you'll need to sign back in afterward.

4. Disable Browser Extensions

Ad blockers, privacy extensions, and PDF viewers are the most frequent culprits here — they can interfere with how Google Sheets loads scripts in the background.

  1. Disable all your extensions at once.
  2. Reload Google Sheets and see if it connects normally.
  3. If it works, re-enable your extensions one at a time, reloading after each, until you find the one causing the conflict.

5. Sign Out and Back In to Your Google Account

Account-level glitches are common, and a simple sign-out/sign-in cycle resolves a surprising number of them. This is also worth trying if you use multiple Google accounts — you may be signed into the wrong one for the sheet you're trying to reach.

6. Check Sharing Permissions

If you're trying to open a sheet someone shared with you, the issue might not be a "connection" problem at all — it could simply be a permissions problem.

  • Confirm the file's sharing setting isn't set to Restricted when it needs to allow your account.
  • Double-check you're signed into the exact Google account the file was shared with.
  • Ask the file owner to confirm your access level if you're still locked out.

Still Not Connecting?

Try a different browser or device entirely. If the sheet also fails to load there, the problem likely isn't local to your machine — it could be a wider Google service issue or a firewall/network-level restriction (common on school or office networks). Check Google's official Workspace Status Dashboard to rule out an outage before troubleshooting further.

Mobile App: A Slightly Different Checklist

If you're on the Google Sheets mobile app rather than a browser, start with the basics that don't apply to desktop: make sure the app is updated to the latest version from the Play Store or App Store, and try clearing the app's cache directly through your phone's Settings → Apps → Sheets → Storage menu.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Google Sheets say "Trying to connect" and never load?

This almost always points to a network issue, either an unstable connection on your end or, less often, a temporary problem with Google's servers. Reloading and checking your connection resolves it in most cases.

Can a browser extension really block Google Sheets?

Yes. Ad blockers and privacy-focused extensions sometimes block scripts that Google Sheets relies on to load properly, even though they're not specifically targeting Google Sheets.

Do I need an internet connection to use Google Sheets?

Yes, unless you've specifically enabled offline access ahead of time through Google Drive settings. Without that setup, an active connection is required to open or edit any file.
