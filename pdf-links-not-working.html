Google Sheets PDF Links Not Working? Here's How to Fix It

A PDF link in a Google Sheets cell that suddenly stops working — or never worked for some collaborators in the first place — is a common and confusing problem. Since Google Sheets doesn't store PDFs directly, every linked PDF depends on something outside the spreadsheet itself, which is exactly where things tend to break. Here are the most likely causes, in order of how often they actually turn out to be the problem.

1. Sharing Permissions Changed on the PDF

This is by far the most common cause. The link itself hasn't changed, but access to the file it points to has.

  1. Open Google Drive and locate the PDF.
  2. Right-click it and select Share.
  3. Check the General access setting — if it says Restricted, only people individually added can open it.
  4. Change it to Anyone with the link, with the role set to Viewer.
If the link works for you but not for coworkers, this is almost always the cause — you likely have direct access as the file's owner or an added collaborator, while others do not.

2. The PDF Was Moved or Deleted

Google Drive links are tied to a file's unique ID, not a folder path — so moving a file between folders won't break its link. Deleting it, or having someone else delete it, will. Check the Drive trash; if the file is there, restoring it usually restores the link immediately, since the ID hasn't changed.

3. Links Break When You Export the Sheet to PDF

This one catches people off guard: exporting a Google Sheet itself to PDF (File → Download → PDF) can strip out hyperlinks from cells in the resulting document, even though the links work perfectly fine inside Google Sheets. This is a known limitation of the export process, not something broken in your specific file.

Workaround

If recipients need clickable links, share the live Google Sheet itself (set to view-only) instead of a PDF export of it, or manually add the URLs as plain visible text so they're readable even without a working hyperlink.

4. The Link Uses a Non-Drive Hosting Service

If the PDF is hosted somewhere other than Google Drive — Dropbox, a company server, or a document-sharing tool — Google Sheets won't create the automatic "smart chip" preview that Drive links get, and any outage or permission change on that external service will break the link independently of anything in your spreadsheet.

5. Browser Extensions Blocking the Link

Ad blockers and privacy extensions occasionally interfere with file preview pop-ups. Test the link in an incognito window with extensions disabled — if it opens there, an extension is the cause.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did my PDF links work before but stop working now?

This is almost always a sharing permissions change on the PDF's Drive folder, since links themselves rarely change on their own once created.

Do PDF links still work after exporting a Google Sheet to PDF?

Not reliably. Exporting a spreadsheet to PDF frequently strips hyperlinks from cells, which is a known limitation rather than a bug you introduced.

Why does the link work for me but not for coworkers?

This points to a permissions issue. The PDF is likely still set to Restricted access, or shared only with you individually rather than with your whole team or organization.
